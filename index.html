Tool 01

The 1:1 Walkthrough

Use this as a live guide during your fortnightly 1:1. Four phases. What to say, what to listen for, and what to avoid.

i.
Open

Make space

  • "How are you?"
  • "What's on your mind or heart?"
  • Pause. Let them fill the silence.
Watch out forJumping in with your own agenda. Rushing past "how are you" as a formality.
ii.
Dig

Find the real thing

  • "And what else?"
  • "What's the most challenging part for you?"
  • Reflect briefly between questions.
Watch out forOffering solutions too early. Interpreting instead of reflecting.
iii.
Unlock

Hand it back

  • "What ideas do you have?"
  • "What do you want?"
  • "If you say yes, what do you say no to?"
Watch out forGiving your answer disguised as a question. Rescuing when they hesitate.
iv.
Close

Anchor the learning

  • "How can I help?"
  • "What was most valuable for you?"
  • Log notes in the Consultant File.
Watch out forSaying yes to everything. Skipping the final question — it's what anchors the learning.
Tool 02

What Am I Dealing With?

When something isn't right, use this to decide your next move. Diagnose first. Move second.

What you're seeing
What it probably is
Your move
Misses the point of tasks even after explanation. Doesn't grasp the role.
GWC: "Get It" missing
Clarify the role. Better PD, break tasks down, offer training. If it persists after support, consider role adjustment or a developmental plan.
Capable but disengaged. Avoiding tasks. No initiative or enthusiasm.
GWC: "Want It" missing
Explore motivation in a 1:1. Discuss career aspirations. Check if the role still fits. Consider enrichment or a move.
Wants to succeed but keeps making errors, missing deadlines, or producing subpar work.
GWC: "Capacity" missing
Targeted training and coaching. Adjust workload. Check if resources are adequate.
A specific behaviour is off — tardiness, missed agreements, attitude — but it's recent or occasional.
Gradient: early stage
Notice silently first. If it repeats, inquire privately: "Is everything alright?" Assume innocence.
You've already had the conversation and the behaviour hasn't changed.
Gradient: escalating
Offer support, restate expectations explicitly, log an AFI. If it still persists, move to Management Action (IR, formal warning, or PIP).
Performance has dropped significantly across multiple areas and informal coaching hasn't worked.
Active Management
Intervene directly. Audit their tasks, stabilise immediate risks, create a short-term "Get Well" plan with daily check-ins for 1–2 weeks.
Overwhelmed, stressed, or burning out — but the work quality is still there.
Coaching moment
Use the questions in your 1:1. This isn't a performance issue — it's a leadership moment. Help them find boundaries and energy.
Rule of thumb

Start with a question, not a directive.
